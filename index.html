Julia Carolyn Child


Julia Child (1912-2004) is one of the most well-known and well-loved American television icons of the 20th century. Pioneer of a new television genre, the home-cooking show, French Cuisine evangelist, and unabashed feminist, she showed the country what it is to be a woman, to be a cook, and to be yourself.

Year of birth She was born the 15th August, 1912 in Pasadena, California, U.S.

Our Items

These 10 items represent Julia Child's culinary career and legacy throughout the last half of the 20th century. The rise of Julia also saw the rise of television, mass media, second-wave feminism, and the Cold War. These 10 objects paint a broader and interconnected picture of twentieth-century America and Julia Child's place in it.

Metadata alignment

The Smithsonian Institutions use the proprietary "Index Metadata Model", a mix of different standards including Dublin Core, MARC, METS, and elements from their cataloging software. We selected Dublin Core to stand in for the Index Metadata Model since our items from the Smithsonian Institutions were all museum objects. We did not include Darwin Core in the alignment since it is highly specific to living organisms and not applicable to 9 of the 10 items.

Conceptual model

The conceptual model uses 21 different ontologies from a variety of disciplines. In addition to FRBR, CIDOC CRM, and Dublin Core, we also used Darwin Core, the Plant Trait Ontology, BBC's Food and Programmes ontologies, and the EduProgression ontology, among others. The model is made with Graffoo.
